NLPPythonTF-IDFNMF

Natural Language Interpreter for Classification

March 2023

For the company Ternium, an NLP solution was developed to address the challenge of analyzing thousands of machinery failure reports written in free-text format, which had inconsistencies, errors, and non-standardized terminology.

Methodology & Results:

  • An unsupervised learning model was applied, as there was no previously classified data.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques were used to clean and standardize the text, including the NLTK library and regular expressions (Regex).
  • The text was transformed into numerical vectors using the **TF-IDF** algorithm to measure the importance of words in the reports.
  • **NMF (Non-negative Matrix Factorization)** was used to reduce dimensionality and group the reports into thematic clusters, identifying the most common types of failures.
  • The final model successfully identified **11 distinct groups** or types of failures, allowing Ternium to better understand the root causes of operational problems and optimize their maintenance processes.

Gravitational Wave Detection using TDA

Using Topological Data Analysis (TDA) to classify gravitational wave signals, offering an efficient alternative to deep learning.

TDAPythonGiotto-tdaScikit-learn

Gravitational Wave Detection using TDA

Academic Project

This project explores the use of Topological Data Analysis (TDA) as a novel and computationally efficient method to detect the presence of gravitational waves in time series with high background noise.

Methodology & Results:

  • 10,000 synthetic signals were generated, some with embedded gravitational waves and others with pure noise, to train and evaluate the model.
  • **Takens' Embedding** was applied to reconstruct the dynamics of the signals in a higher-dimensional space, followed by **Principal Component Analysis (PCA)** for dimensionality reduction.
  • Using the **giotto-tda** library, **persistent homology** diagrams were calculated to extract the topological features of each signal.
  • Finally, a **Logistic Regression** classifier was trained with the extracted topological features, achieving an **Accuracy of 0.777** and an Area Under the Curve **(AUC) of 0.85**.

Tour Route Optimization for Tourists

June 2023

This project focused on solving the tourist route planning problem to maximize traveler satisfaction, subject to time and budget constraints. It was applied to a case study in the city of Puebla, Mexico.

Methodology & Results:

  • The problem was modeled as a variant of the **Orienteering Problem with Time Windows (OPTW)**, ideal for situations where the reward (satisfaction) is more important than visiting all nodes.
  • The **GAMS (General Algebraic Modeling System)** software, a high-level tool for mathematical modeling and optimization, was used.
  • The model considers parameters such as place ratings (satisfaction), opening and closing times, entry costs, and travel times for each point of interest.
  • Optimal routes were successfully generated for different scenarios (7, 12, and 17 locations), demonstrating the model's ability to maximize the total satisfaction score without exceeding the tourist's time or budget.

Air Quality Analysis in Nuevo León

Multivariate AnalysisFactor AnalysisLDAR Studio

Air Quality Analysis in Nuevo León

September 2023

This project, in collaboration with the Integrated Environmental Monitoring System (SIMA), focuses on understanding the complex factors affecting air quality in Monterrey, Nuevo León, and developing a model to predict its status.

Methodology & Results:

  • **Factor Analysis** was used to reduce the dimensionality of 16 variables (pollutants and meteorological), identifying 4 key latent factors that explain 64% of the data's variability.
  • The identified factors were: Meteorological Variables, Atmospheric Pollutants, Climatological Factors, and Time (seasons/hours).
  • A **Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA)** model was built using these factors to classify air quality into 5 categories (Good, Acceptable, Poor, etc.).
  • The final classifier achieved a **70% accuracy**, proving to be an effective tool for prediction and potential public health decision-making. The analysis environment was **R Studio**.

Image Classification with CNN vs. CapsNet

September 2023

This project involved the implementation and comparative evaluation of two neural network architectures for image classification, using the well-known Fashion MNIST dataset.

Models & Results:

  • Model 1 (CNN): A standard Convolutional Neural Network was built, achieving an accuracy of **92.25%**.
  • Model 2 (CapsNet): A more novel architecture, a Capsule Network, was implemented, which obtained an accuracy of **88.25%** but with a significantly lower average loss (0.096%) and much faster training time (10 min vs. 1 hour).
  • The project's conclusion was that **CapsNet proved to be the better overall model** due to its excellent balance of accuracy, low loss, and computational efficiency.
